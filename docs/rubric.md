# Singularity Quality Rubric

*10 dimensions used to validate and score the Singularity plan before build. Target: 90+/100.*

---

## The Rubric

### Dimension 1: Industry Alignment (10 pts)

**Question:** Does this align with where the software delivery industry is heading in 2026?

**What "10/10" looks like:**
- Directly addresses the AI-native agency trend (Cognition, Devin, GitHub Copilot Workspace, etc.)
- Maps to client expectations that have shifted post-GPT-4
- Positions ahead of, not behind, the industry curve
- Acknowledges where current tools fall short (context loss, coordination gaps)

**Scoring criteria:**
- 9-10: Clearly ahead of current industry direction; would be featured in a16z / YC roundup
- 7-8: Aligned with current trends; not leading-edge but well-positioned
- 5-6: Somewhat aligned; misses some key industry signals
- <5: Out of touch or fighting the trend

---

### Dimension 2: Call Fidelity (10 pts)

**Question:** Does this accurately reflect the decisions, insights, and vision from the Spartan Review meeting?

**What "10/10" looks like:**
- SpartanBrain's $82/month insight is prominently featured
- The 70/30 human/agent split is clearly articulated
- FileFelicity demo scenario is usable
- The billing model innovation (tokens + story points + infra) is front and center
- Miguel, Sebastian, Jorge, Amilkar, Carlos are all represented correctly

**Scoring criteria:**
- 9-10: Every major decision from the call is reflected; nothing significant missed
- 7-8: Core decisions captured; some details missing
- 5-6: Main theme captured; specifics lost
- <5: Diverges significantly from what was discussed

---

### Dimension 3: Game Changer Potential (10 pts)

**Question:** Could this genuinely change how software agencies operate at industry scale?

**What "10/10" looks like:**
- Addresses a structural problem (not just a workflow improvement)
- Has a clear path to 10x improvement in speed and cost
- Creates new revenue streams that didn't exist before
- Could be adopted by agencies globally, not just SingularAgency
- Has a network effect or moat built in

**Scoring criteria:**
- 9-10: Category-defining; creates new industry segment
- 7-8: Significant improvement; would spread through the industry over 2-3 years
- 5-6: Meaningful but incremental; enhances existing model
- <5: Feature-level improvement; easily copied

---

### Dimension 4: Shortest Change Loop Implementation (10 pts)

**Question:** Does the plan concretely implement the "shortest change loop" from client intent to shipped product?

**What "10/10" looks like:**
- Every step from client speaks → ship is defined and automated
- Handoff points between AI and human are explicitly mapped
- No ambiguity about where a feature goes when it enters the system
- The loop is demonstrable in the scrollytelling site

**Scoring criteria:**
- 9-10: Full loop implemented; demo-able end to end
- 7-8: Loop defined; a few steps still theoretical
- 5-6: Partial loop; major gaps in the chain
- <5: Loop described but not implemented

---

### Dimension 5: Monetization Revolution (10 pts)

**Question:** Does the billing model represent a genuine revolution in how agencies charge for software?

**What "10/10" looks like:**
- Three new revenue streams clearly articulated (tokens, infra, expertise)
- Side-by-side comparison makes the economics undeniable
- The agency's incentive structure aligns with client outcomes
- Token charging is positioned as transparent, not exploitative

**Scoring criteria:**
- 9-10: Billing model would cause a "wait, we could charge for that?" reaction
- 7-8: Clear improvement over current model; clients would understand immediately
- 5-6: Somewhat different; doesn't fundamentally change the economics
- <5: Same old model with AI as a cost center

---

### Dimension 6: Visual WOW Factor (10 pts)

**Question:** Does the demo site create genuine visual and interactive moments that make audiences say "wow"?

**What "10/10" looks like:**
- The River demo works live (real Claude API call, streaming)
- At least 3 scroll sections have genuine WOW moments
- Animations are purposeful — they demonstrate the product, not just look nice
- A non-technical PO understands the value within 90 seconds of scrolling
- Mobile responsive

**Scoring criteria:**
- 9-10: Every section has a moment; demo feels like a product, not a pitch deck
- 7-8: Most sections wow; a few are just text
- 5-6: One or two wow moments; rest is informational
- <5: Looks good but doesn't demonstrate anything

---

### Dimension 7: Agent-Human Model Clarity (10 pts)

**Question:** Is it clear when agents work, when humans work, and how they collaborate?

**What "10/10" looks like:**
- The 70/30 rule is explained with specific examples
- The `agent` / `human` story label system is demonstrated
- Mike and Runly's specific responsibilities are distinct and clear
- Escalation path (when agent hands off to human) is shown
- No false promise that agents replace humans entirely

**Scoring criteria:**
- 9-10: Crystal clear; a client PO could explain it to their CEO after one scroll
- 7-8: Mostly clear; model is defined but some nuance lost
- 5-6: Conceptually there; implementation details missing
- <5: Vague; could mean anything

---

### Dimension 8: Knowledge Architecture Depth (10 pts)

**Question:** Does the Totem system represent a durable, tool-agnostic knowledge moat?

**What "10/10" looks like:**
- Four Totems are fully defined (client.md, sold.md, design-system.md, network.md)
- The "tool-agnostic" argument is made clearly ("HyperAgent gets replaced; Totems remain")
- Generation process (from GitHub URL + website scan) is demonstrated
- Maintenance mechanism (The River keeps them current) is shown
- Why knowledge architecture is the real moat, not the AI tool

**Scoring criteria:**
- 9-10: Totems feel like IP; something an agency would pay to have built
- 7-8: Solid architecture; value proposition is clear
- 5-6: Good concept; not fully differentiated from a wiki or Notion
- <5: Just documentation; no clear moat

---

### Dimension 9: Synthetic Jump Narrative (10 pts)

**Question:** Does the storytelling make someone feel the magnitude of the shift — that this is not incremental improvement but a category jump?

**What "10/10" looks like:**
- The phrase "synthetic evolutionary jump" is explained and felt, not just stated
- The contrast between the old world (meeting → PM → story → sprint → ship) and new world is visceral
- The $82/month SpartanBrain vs $5,000/month PM comparison lands emotionally
- By the end of the scroll, the reader feels the industry has changed and they need to catch up

**Scoring criteria:**
- 9-10: Narrative makes you want to call your agency and ask why they're not doing this
- 7-8: Compelling case; clear value
- 5-6: Informative; reads like a product page
- <5: Dry; misses the emotional resonance

---

### Dimension 10: Platform Vision (10 pts)

**Question:** Does the plan establish a clear path from today's demo to a multi-client, multi-agency platform?

**What "10/10" looks like:**
- Year 1 → 2 → 3 trajectory is clear
- Platform economics (each new client is cheaper to serve) are articulated
- The marketplace / ecosystem vision is present without being vaporware
- Singularity is positioned as infrastructure, not a product feature

**Scoring criteria:**
- 9-10: You can see the billion-dollar roadmap from the demo
- 7-8: Platform potential is clear; path is outlined
- 5-6: Good for one agency; platform story is weak
- <5: Looks like a one-client tool

## How to Use This Rubric

1. Score each dimension independently (no peeking at adjacent scores)
2. Any dimension below 7 is a blocker — must be addressed before shipping the demo
3. Target: 90+/100 before Carlos begins the 6-week sprint
4. Re-score after each major revision
5. The rubric scores the **plan and demo**, not the final product — the final product gets its own rubric
